Description


The epiDAMIK workshop has been held annually at KDD from 2018 (under the name “Epidemiology meets Data Mining and Knowledge discovery”), consistently attracting an interdisciplinary community working at the intersection of AI, data mining, and population health.

The workshop's aim is to energize the use and development of next-generation AI and data systems for public health, understood as the collective societal effort to assure healthy conditions through policy, and for population health, which centers on how healthcare systems and organizations coordinate to improve outcomes for their communities. The workshop emphasizes enhancing our ability to simulate, interpret, and guide decision-making in complex health systems. This year’s edition highlights generative models, intelligent agents, causal inference, and hybrid approaches (AI + scientific modeling). From chronic disease management (e.g., obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular disease) and environmental health risks (e.g., pollution, extreme heat, wildfire smoke) to the substance use epidemics, mental health crises, and disparities in healthcare access, today’s health challenges are shaped by intricate behavioral, biological, ecological, and social dynamics. Infectious diseases add another layer of urgency; tuberculosis remains a leading global cause of death, and recent outbreaks of H1N3, Ebola, and measles have underscored the need for scalable systems that can anticipate and respond to epidemics. Recent advances in AI and data mining offer the promise of capturing heterogeneous behaviors, simulating counterfactual interventions, and supporting robust policy planning under uncertainty. By bridging disciplinary boundaries and highlighting emerging techniques, our workshop will catalyze progress in how we design and deploy AI and data mining to support a healthier, more resilient society.
